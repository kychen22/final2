The Cold War

Visual arts took a turn during the Cold War, with propaganda becoming a battleground between the Soviet Union and the United States. In this highly-charged time, a number of other arts also grew political.

Music

Popular American music was displayed worldwide as an attempt to win over allies. Jazz, especially, was seen as a representative of the American ideology due to its improvisatory nature. Furthermore, increasingly political songs arose during this time, including Billy Joel's "We Didn't Start the Fire", pictured above.
